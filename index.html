My name is Evelyn, and Im the proud grandmother of six beautiful grandchildren.

A few years ago, I began to notice that my hearing was gradually deteriorating.

At first, it was the subtle sounds of nature that I missed my morning walks used to be peppered with the gentle rustling of leaves, the trickling of water in the nearby creek, and the melodic songs of birds little signs of life, I called them.

But over the years, those little signs of life started going silent. My morning walks started to feel empty and lifeless, and I eventually stopped taking them completely.

Eventually, my hearing loss started affecting my time with my family and friends. I struggled to hear the laughter of my grandchildren as they played in the yard (my absolute favorite signs of life) and I no longer felt comfortable participating in the lively conversations around the dinner table. My hearing loss made me feel more like an observer than part of the family.

I used to chat with my best friend Mary for hours every Sunday, but the calls became more difficult as I lost my hearing. After one call ended after just 20 minutes, I remember feeling a deep sense of loss for the easy, carefree conversations I could no longer have with her or anyone.

I got tired of asking people to repeat themselves leaning across the table being told I was yelling without realizing it worrying about causing an accident my world became increasingly quiet and isolated, and I felt a profound sense of loss.

Of course, I tried to get help. My doctor agreed that I had hearing loss and he sent me to a specialist to try hearing aids.

I tried a pair that worked at the doctors office, despite being bulky and a bit difficult to put on. However, because my hearing loss wasnt as severe as other cases and after months of stressful negotiations, my insurance denied my claim to cover custom hearing aids.

And at this stage in my life, I just didnt have the $5,000 I needed to cover the cost out of pocket

I felt trapped in hearing loss purgatory. Every moment of verbal communication became stressful and embarrassing but I wasnt deaf enough to get proper help.
